Search your inbox by a hunch

1

Connect a mailbox

Any IMAP account — Proton, Gmail, Outlook. Credentials encrypted at rest.

2

Indexed on-device

A background worker syncs it locally and embeds every message on your own machine — nothing sent to an embedding API.

3

Ask in plain language

"The document the bank sent about my mortgage." A Claude agent searches, and asks a question if it's ambiguous.

4

Read the result

Open and view the emails it finds. That's it — Hunch never sends or manages mail.

What it is — and isn't

It is

  • Semantic, conversational search over your own mail
  • Local-first — emails on your disk, embeddings on-device
  • Multi-user, each with their own Anthropic key
  • A read-only viewer for the emails it finds

It isn't

  • An email client
  • Sending or replying to mail
  • Folder management or inbox triage
  • A hosted service that reads your mail in the cloud

How it's built

Local-first & private

Meilisearch's HuggingFace embedder vectorizes every message locally with a multilingual model — an English query finds a French email, and your mail never leaves the box to be indexed. Only your query and a few snippets reach Claude.

Async & real-time

Sync and search run in a background worker; progress and results are pushed to the browser live over a Mercure hub, so the page never freezes on a long agent run. Built on Symfony and the Symfony AI components.
